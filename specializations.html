Nursing is an extremely diverse field with dozens of sub-disciplines and specializations. This can be a daunting prospect if you are just starting your nursing education. Use this resource to learn more about each specialization.
Name Description Minimum Education Level Salary*

Medical-Surgical Nurse

 You will provide care to adult patients in a hospital setting who are suffering from acute or chronic illess or recovering from surgery. This is one of the largest sub-disciplines of nursing with over 650,000 RNs. RN or Bachelors $57,000 - $62,000

Ambulatory Care Nurse

 You will work mostly outside of the hospital setting and provide care for illnesses and injuries. Your main focus will be on general health and pain management. RN or Bachelors $62,000 - $75,000

Critical-Care Nurse

 You will treat patients through out the life cycle who are experiencing a critical illness or injury that will likely require intensive interventions. Sometimes also referred to as ICU nurse. Masters $45,000 - $75,000

Heart Failure Nurse

 You will improve outcomes for patients with heart failure in hospital or ambulatory settings. Bachelors $72,000 - $89,000

Neuroscience Nurse

 You will provide care for a wide variety of patients suffering from illnesses or injuries that cause neurological damage. Work environments include hospitals, rehabilitation centers, and brain injury units. RN or Bachelors $58,000 - $85,000

Nephrology Nurse

 You will care for patients at risk for, or experiencing kidney disease in a wide variety of settings. You may assist preventative care, dialysis, or post-operative transplant care. RN or Bachelors $52,000 - $83,000

Nurse Executive

 You will serve as part of the leadership team for your organization by managing employees, shaping policies, and maintaining budgets in addition to other executive-level duties. Bachelors, Masters, or PhD/DNP $60,000 - $250,000

Perianesthesia Nurse

 You will provide care to patients pre- and post-anesthesia and provide intervention in case of adverse effects. Also referred to as a Recovery Room Nurse. RN or Bachelors $50,000 - $75,000

Plastic Surgery Nurse

 You will assist surgeons during plastic surgery procedures and provide pre- and post-operative care to patients recovering from surgery. RN or Bachelors $45,000 - $86,000

 Perioperative Registered Nurse

 You will work with surgeons and anesthesiologists to provide care for patients before, during and after surgery. You may serve as a liaison between the surgery team and the family of the patient. RN or Bachelors $48,000 - $80,000

 Developmental Disabilities Nurse

 You will work in a wide variety of settings to care for individuals with special needs as well as providing support to the patient's family. Also known as a Special Needs Nurse. LPN, RN or Bachelors $34,000 - $60,000

 Emergency Nurse

 You will care for patients of all ages with a variety of illnesses and injuries during emergency situations. RN or Bachelors $62,000 - $78,000

Forensic Nurse

 You will treat patients who have experienced trauma, primarily in an Emergency Room setting. You will use your criminal justice and medical evidence training to help investigate violent crimes and traumatic injuries. You may be asked to provide evidence in court. Masters $50,000 - $140,000

Neonatal Nurse

 You will care for newborn infants who may have been born premature, with birth defects, or other afflictions in a hospital setting. RN or Bachelors $45,000 - $120,000

Oncology Nurse

 You will provide care for cancer patients in a variety of settings. Bachelors $44,000 - $78,000

 Urologic Nurse

 You will provide care and preventative services for patients experiencing and at risk for diseases of the urinary system. RN or Bachelors $69,000 - $76,000

 Wound Ostomy and Continence Nurse

 You will work in a variety of healthcare settings to treat patients with acute and chronic wounds, an ostomy (surgical opening for discharge of bodily waste), or with continence issues. You will likely work with a healthcare team. Bachelors or Masters $70,000 - $100,000
 Air & Surface Transport Nurse You will administer care to patients being transported to or between healthcare facilities. RN or Bachelors $45,000 - $59,000

Nurse Practitioner

 You will serve as the primary care provider for patients by performing preventative care and addressing acute and chronic illnesses and injuries. Depending on your state, you may be required to practice under a physician. Masters $63,000 - $135,000

Occupational Health Nurses

 You will investigate, prevent, and treat workplace-related injuries and illnesses. Bachelors $73,000 - $90,000

Holistic Nurse

 You will use alternative medicine treatments such as acupuncture, massage, aromatherapy, etc. to treat patients. This approach is sometimes combined with traditional Western medicine. Bachelors or Masters $44,000 - $71,000

 Psychiatric Nurse

 You will treat patients with mental illnesses by assessing their needs, creating treatment plans, implementing treatments, and assessing the effectiveness of interventions. Bachelors $59,000 - $89,000

Pain Management Nurse

 You will provide care for patients experience either acute or chronic pain in a variety of settings. Some of your duties may include providing education to patients on strategies for pain management and working with a health care team to create and implement a treatment plan. Bachelors  $45,000 - $75,000

AIDS Care Nurse

 You will work in a variety of settings to care for patients with HIV/AIDS and educate communities on prevention and treatment. RN or Bachelors $46,000 - $78,000

Pediatric Hematology/Oncology Nurse

 You will treat pediatric patients with blood disorders resulting from cancer or other diseases. You will perform exams, review lab work, and provide diagnoses in the normal course of your duties. RN or Bachelors $68,000 - $88,000

 Women's Health, Obstetric and Neonatal Nurse

 You will provide reproductive care for women throughout the lifecycle but especially through pregnancy, labor, and childbirth. You will also educate women about sexual and physical health topics such as STDs, contraception, and breast wellness. RN or Bachelors $34,000 - $82,000

Hospice and Palliative Nurse

 You will provide physical and emotional support to terminally ill patients and their families during their final days.  RN or Bachelors $47,000 - $79,000

 Pediatric Nurse Practitioner

 You will serve as the primary care provider for patients from infants to young adults by performing preventative care and addressing acute and chronic illnesses and injuries. Depending on your state, you may be required to practice under a physician. Masters  $69,000 - $114,000

 Gerontological Nurse

 You will work with elderly patients to manage illness and pain in a variety of settings such as nursing homes and hospice facilities. RN or Bachelors  $59,000 - $75,000

 Nurse Anesthetist

  You will collaborate with other health professionals to administer anesthesia to patients. You will care for patients before, during, and after anesthesia. Masters $157,000 - $214,000
